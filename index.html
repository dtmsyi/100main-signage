Package/Delivery Signage Initiative

The Problem

Current signage reads "Parcel/Package" with a rightward arrow. Drivers interpret this as "enter building, then turn right," prompting intercom calls for access. Residents report this happens primarily with Amazon drivers‚Äînot FedEx, UPS, or USPS, who run the same routes daily and know the building. Amazon drivers work different routes with 150+ stops per day, meaning unclear signage becomes a request for help. Ambiguous directions turn into resident interruptions.

The Solution

"DO NOT" commands stop unwanted behavior before it starts‚Äîno interpretation needed. The sign structure follows proven wayfinding principles: prohibition first ("DO NOT USE INTERCOM"), then audience ("Amazon/Deliveries"), then correct action ("Use Exterior Door"). Like stop signs and airport signage‚Äîwhen confusion costs time and disrupts residents, clear and unambiguous is the directive.
